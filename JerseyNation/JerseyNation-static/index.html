Welcome to JerseyNation

JerseyNation is a web application project designed as an online store for national team jerseys related to the 2026 World Cup. The platform was created to combine e-commerce structure, data organization and business-oriented analytics in a clean and modern interface.

Project idea

The main idea of this project is to simulate an online jersey store where users can explore products from different national teams, compare jersey options and view product details in an organized and visually appealing way. The project focuses on World Cup 2026 related merchandise and presents a strong foundation for a future complete e-commerce solution. A key differentiator of the platform is its focus not only on standard jersey sales, but also on rare national team jerseys, signed pieces and authentic items worn in World Cup matches and training sessions.

Purpose

The purpose of JerseyNation is to demonstrate how a Razor Pages application can support a realistic business case. It connects front-end design, product presentation and analytics into a single academic project. The website was structured to show how an online store can be organized while also leaving room for future database integration, inventory control and restricted administrative features.

Key features

Jersey catalog

The store presents jerseys by national team and includes important product attributes such as version, color and audience. It also aims to offer premium items such as rare jerseys, autographed pieces and match-worn or training-worn products.

Search and browsing

Users can browse the available products and search for jerseys by team, style or category, making the shopping experience clearer and more user-friendly.

Business analytics

The charts page supports the analytical side of the project by displaying sample business metrics such as sales, product interest and performance trends.

Project scope

At this stage, JerseyNation works as a structured prototype that introduces the website concept, highlights the main pages and demonstrates how the final system can evolve. Future improvements may include an AI-based simulator that allows customers to visualize themselves wearing a jersey, APIs for connection with suppliers and delivery companies, automated sales through bots, and an affiliate program. Additional future developments may also include marketing data integration, cookie-based website analytics, and connections with Google tools to support tracking, performance measurement and digital marketing decisions.
